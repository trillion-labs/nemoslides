# Setting the Frame

The single question this deck answers:

> **Are we expanding profitably and with discipline?**

Our obligation is not growth at any cost — it is **unit-level proof before portfolio-level scale**.

Three things you will see today:

- **Financial results** grounded in retail operating metrics, not narrative
- **Expansion progress** measured against the plan you approved — including shortfalls
- **H2 priorities and guardrails** that keep us honest

| Daypart Mix | — | Breakfast/brunch share up 140 bps; dinner share down 90 bps |
| Channel Mix | — | Delivery now 22% of SSS revenue (vs. 19% H1 2023); in-store 61%; catering 17% |

**Mature-store SSS: +4.6%** · **2023-vintage SSS: +1.2%** · **H1-2024 vintage: N/A**

Weekday lunch softness is structural, not cyclical. We are adjusting labor scheduling and testing a $9.99 weekday combo in 8 stores starting August.

# Headwinds We Are Not Dismissing

Transparency requires naming what could erode the thesis:

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer trade-down accelerates | Medium | AUV and ticket pressure | Value architecture already in place; combo test in Aug |
| Wage growth outpaces pricing | Medium | 50–80 bps margin compression | Labor scheduling optimization; kiosk rollout in 12 stores by Q4 |
| New-store payback stretches past 30 mo. | Low-Medium | IRR erosion on expansion capital | Strict go/no-go gate at Week 12 of each ramp |
| Delivery platform fees | High | Already 3.2% of revenue | Negotiating direct deals; building own channel (piloting in Boston) |
| Commodity spike (wheat, dairy) | Low | COGS +100–150 bps | 70% of wheat hedged through Q1 2025 |

**No single risk is thesis-breaking. In combination, they could slow margin progress by 100–150 bps. That is the range we are managing within.**

# H2 2024 Priorities & Guardrails

### Priorities
1. **Open 11 of 11 planned stores on time and on budget** — no forced locations; walk if metrics don't clear
2. **Get 2023-vintage stores to 18%+ store EBITDA by year-end** — operational playbook, not hope
3. **Resolve Dallas and Portland** — 90-day action plans with defined exit criteria; if not met, we discuss closure/restructuring at Q4 board meeting
4. **Launch delivery direct-to-consumer channel in Boston** — prove P&L uplift before any national rollout
5. **Reduce net leverage to <2.0x** — clear path; requires EBITDA run-rate and disciplined capex

### Guardrails
| Guardrail | Threshold | Action if Breached |
|---|---|---|
| New-store payback | >30 months at month 18 | Pause new site commits; audit site-selection model |
| Store-level EBITDA margin (aggregate) | <18.0% for 2 consecutive quarters | Reduce opening pace to 1 per quarter |
| Free cash flow | Negative for 3+ consecutive quarters | Defer all non-essential capex; remodel freeze |
| Same-store traffic | Declining >2% for 2 consecutive quarters | Accelerate value combo launch; reassess pricing strategy |

**The guardrails are not aspirational. They are the agreement we make with your capital.**
