# Answer up front

## Short answer: **yes — with tighter pacing where returns are not yet at chain average**

- **Core business remained healthy in H1 2024**
  - Net sales **$96.4M**, up **13.7%**
  - Same-store sales **+4.1%**
  - Adjusted EBITDA **$11.2M**, margin **11.6%** vs. **11.0%** last year

- **Expansion is contributing, not masking weakness**
  - **14 openings**, **2 closures/relocations**, ending at **108 stores**
  - H1 2023 opening cohort at month 12 reached **14.8% four-wall EBITDA margin**
  - Newer markets are growing faster, but at **lower margins** than the mature base

- **Capital discipline held**
  - Operating cash flow **$8.7M** covered total capex of **$8.0M**
  - Lease-adjusted leverage improved to **2.1x** from **2.3x** at year-end 2023
  - **3 sites were deferred** rather than approved at sub-threshold returns

## Bottom line
We are still expanding profitably, but the evidence supports a **measured H2 opening pace**, not acceleration.

# Where H1 was off plan — and what we are doing about it

<div class="text-sm">

| Issue | H1 variance vs. plan | Likely cause | H2 response |
|---|---|---|---|
| New-market labor cost | **+110 bps** | Higher training load, thinner manager bench, lower initial productivity | Slow openings in two markets; add floating managers; tighten opening labor model |
| Occupancy on select sites | **+70 to +120 bps** above underwriting on 4 stores | Smaller urban boxes and higher rent escalators | No new lease signed above year-1 occupancy guardrail; renegotiate where possible |
| Waste / shrink | **+30 bps** | Forecasting and prep complexity in early ramp stores | Simplify opening SKU set; daily prep tracking for first 16 weeks |
| Two legacy store closures | Below acceptable cash contribution | Structural traffic/access issues, not temporary demand softness | Redeploy labor and capex to adjacent trade areas with higher returns |

</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-sm">
<strong>Important:</strong> these are operating issues, not balance-sheet or liquidity issues. They are correctable, but they justify a more selective H2 pace.
</div>

# H2 2024 priorities

## 1) Protect base-store productivity
- Deliver **same-store sales of +3% to +4%**
- Hold gross margin at **58.5% or better**
- Keep chain four-wall EBITDA margin at **15%+**

## 2) Tighten new-store ramp performance
- Limit H2 openings to **8–10 stores**
- Require week-12 sales at **90%+ of pro forma**
- Keep build cost per opening at **$0.45M or less**

## 3) Reduce variability in newer markets
- Lower labor as a percent of sales by **~100 bps**
- Reduce waste by **30 bps**
- Improve manager retention and training coverage before adding density

## 4) Preserve cash and flexibility
- Maintain **positive free cash flow after maintenance capex**
- Keep lease-adjusted leverage at **2.25x or below**
- Avoid traffic buys that dilute store contribution margin

# H2 guardrails and decision rules

<div class="text-sm">

| Decision area | Guardrail | If breached |
|---|---|---|
| Site approval | Year-1 occupancy cost must be **≤ 11%** of projected sales | Do not sign lease |
| Store underwriting | Cash payback must be **≤ 30 months** | Defer until economics improve |
| New-market sequencing | First two stores in a market must deliver **≥ 13% four-wall EBITDA margin** for two consecutive quarters before a third opens | Pause additional openings |
| Build cost control | Build cost inflation above **+7% vs. budget** must be offset by TI, rent, or sales upside | Rebid or defer |
| Store health | Any store below **10% four-wall EBITDA margin** for two consecutive quarters enters a formal fix-or-exit process | 60-day action plan |
| Balance sheet | Lease-adjusted leverage must remain **≤ 2.25x** | Expansion limited to already committed openings |

</div>

## Closing view
**Yes, Golden Crust is expanding profitably — but the right H2 posture is disciplined continuation, not acceleration.**  
We will keep growing only where store-level returns, cash payback, and balance-sheet flexibility remain intact.
