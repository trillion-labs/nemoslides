# DataVault B2B Lead Generation Review
## Q1 FY2026 Campaign Performance and Q2 Scale Recommendation

**Audience:** Marketing + Sales Leadership

**Prepared by:** Revenue Marketing

# Executive summary

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-8">

<div>

### What happened in Q1
- We exceeded pipeline creation target despite only modest MQL growth.
- Highest-quality demand came from **search, webinars, SDR outbound, and ABM**.
- **Content syndication delivered volume, but not quality**; it inflated top-of-funnel and absorbed SDR follow-up capacity.
- Performance was strongest in **regulated verticals** and **named / high-fit accounts**.

### Why it matters
- The campaign is **scalable**, but not by simply adding more spend to every channel.
- Next-quarter upside depends on shifting budget toward **high-intent capture** and **account-based activation**.

</div>

<div>

### Q1 topline
- **Spend:** \$820k
- **Leads:** 6,420
- **MQLs:** 1,180
- **SQLs:** 356
- **Opportunities:** 92
- **Pipeline created:** \$5.2M

### Recommendation
Adopt a **balanced scale plan**:
- Increase budget to **\$1.05M**
- Cut low-yield syndication spend by more than half
- Expand search, webinars, ABM, and SDR capacity
- Focus on **FinServ, Healthcare, and SaaS**
- Target **\$7.2M pipeline** next quarter

</div>

# What limited performance this quarter

| Constraint | Evidence | Business impact | Recommended fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low-quality syndication leads | 2,350 leads generated, but only 31 SQLs and 6 opps | SDR capacity diluted; weak lead-to-meeting efficiency | Cut spend by 50%+ and tighten publisher list |
| Slow webinar follow-up | Average sales follow-up was 36 hours vs. 12-hour target | Meeting-set rate dropped on highest-intent event leads | Route hot leads instantly and enforce SLA |
| Generic search landing pages | Non-branded conversion rate lagged vertical pages by ~30% | Lost efficiency on high-intent paid search traffic | Build vertical landing pages for FinServ / Healthcare / SaaS |
| Limited ABM contact coverage | Tier 1 accounts averaged only 42% buying-center coverage | Enterprise deal creation capped by low account depth | Add contact enrichment + intent data |
| Messaging too broad in mid-funnel nurture | Strong click rates, weaker meeting conversion | Engagement did not consistently translate to sales action | Create role-specific nurture by buyer stage |

### Bottom line
The campaign did **not** hit a demand ceiling; it hit an **execution and allocation ceiling**.

- Builds repeatable infrastructure before pursuing a more aggressive budget step-up
