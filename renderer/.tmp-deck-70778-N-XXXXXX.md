## Expansion Pipeline & Capital Discipline

- **Planned openings**: 22 stores in FY 2025 (12 in H1, 10 in H2)  
- **Geographic focus**: high‑traffic urban corridors & underserved secondary markets  
- **Capex allocation**: 68% to construction/design, 32% to technology & automation  
- **Store‑level ROI (IRR)**: 17.5% (target ≥ 15%)  
- **Capex intensity**: $0.68 per store opened (benchmark: industry avg $0.81)  

*Note: All new stores follow the same rollout playbook, ensuring repeatable unit economics.*

---
## Are We Expanding Profitably & With Discipline?  

**Yes.**  
- **Revenue growth** is anchored in **profitable store openings**, not pure capacity addition.  
- **Net margin** has risen **+188 bps** while expanding store count by **14**.  
- **CAC** is below the $13,500 guardrail, and **payback** is within the 14‑month limit.  
- **Same‑store sales** remain positive (+3.2%), indicating the new stores are cannibalizing little existing demand.  

*Bottom line: Each incremental store adds net contribution while preserving overall profitability.*

## H2 Priorities & Guardrails  

| Priority | Action Item | Success Metric |
|----------|-------------|----------------|
| Optimize unit economics | Reduce labor hours per transaction by 3% | Labor cost per transaction ≤ $0.81 |
| Strengthen inventory turns | Implement just‑in‑time ordering | Inventory turnover ↑ to 7.5× |
| Enhance repeat purchase rate | Launch targeted loyalty program | Repeat‑purchase rate ≥ 32% |
| Guardrail on expansion | Cap store openings at **≤ 30** in FY 2025 | No breach of capex‑per‑store budget |
| Reinforce cash discipline | Maintain cash conversion cycle ≤ 45 days | Cash flow stability |

*The focus is on **sustaining margin expansion** while **scaling responsibly**.*
