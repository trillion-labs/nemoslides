# Urban Threads × StockMaster
## From Chronic Stockouts to Predictive Replenishment

A case study for retail operations leaders

# The Situation

Urban Threads — 142 stores, 3 DCs, ~18,000 SKUs

**A fast-growing specialty retailer hitting the wall on inventory execution.**

- Revenue growing 14% YoY, but margins declining for 3 consecutive quarters
- Operations team spending 60% of time on fire-drill replenishment
- No single source of truth across channels (POS, e-comm, wholesale)
- Board mandate: fix inventory efficiency before the next fiscal year

> "We were profitable on paper but bleeding cash in the back room."
> — Maria Salcedo, VP Operations, Urban Threads

---

# The Pain Points

### What Urban Threads was dealing with every day

| Problem | Impact |
|---|---|
| Stockout rate averaging **11.4%** | $8.6M in estimated lost sales per year |
| Overstock on 34% of SKUs | $4.1M in excess carrying costs annually |
| 3–5 day lag on inventory visibility | Reactive ordering, frequent air shipments |
| 22 labor hours/week per store on counts | Pulling associates off the floor during peak |

**The core issue:** decentralized spreadsheets and legacy POS couldn't keep up with omnichannel demand velocity.

---

# What Changed

### StockMaster's role — a workflow redesign, not just a tool swap

Urban Threads didn't just plug in software. They restructured three processes simultaneously:

1. **Unified data layer** — POS, e-comm, and wholesale feeds consolidated into StockMaster's real-time inventory hub
2. **Demand-driven replenishment** — Store-level min/max replaced top-down allocation; algorithms adjusted weekly by sell-through velocity
3. **Exception-based counts** — Cycle counts triggered by variance alerts instead of blanket schedules

**Implementation timeline:** 14 weeks from kickoff to full rollout across all 142 locations.

# Lessons from the Journey

### What made the difference — and what almost didn't

**What worked:**
- Executive sponsor (VP Ops) owned the KPIs from day one — no ambiguity on success criteria
- Pilot in 12 stores for 6 weeks before rolling out, with store managers co-designing the replenishment rules
- Integration completed *before* training began — associates never saw a broken workflow

**What they'd do differently:**
- Invested earlier in DC process alignment — the first 4 weeks of rollout revealed picking inconsistencies that StockMaster couldn't fix alone
- Set realistic expectations on carrying cost reduction — initial projection was $2.1M, actual was $1.9M; the gap was seasonal markdown reserves

---

# Your Takeaway

### If you're facing similar inventory challenges, three things to act on now:

1. **Audit your visibility lag.** If you can't see true inventory position across channels in under an hour, you're already ordering blind. This is the single highest-leverage fix.

2. **Replace schedule-based counts with exception-based counts.** Urban Threads cut 68% of count labor by only counting what the system flagged — and accuracy actually *improved*.

3. **Pilot with operations, not IT.** The pilot stores that co-designed replenishment rules hit target KPIs 3 weeks faster than the stores that just received training.
