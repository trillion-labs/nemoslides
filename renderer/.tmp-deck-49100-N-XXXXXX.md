### What improved
- Deployment frequency increased from **2.1/week** in Q1 to **3.4/week** in Q2.
- Rework rate fell from **12%** to **9%**.
- Sev2+ production incidents declined from **11** in Q1 to **7** in Q2.
- MTTR improved from **2.9h** to **1.8h**.

</div>

<div>

### What is limiting us
- Unplanned work rose from **14%** of sprint capacity in Q1 to **23%** in Q2.
- Sprint commitment reliability improved only modestly: **80% → 85%**.
- Debt is concentrated in **Billing Sync** and **Workflow Engine**, which also have the highest change-fail rates.
- CI/build times in hotspot services remain above **28 min**, slowing delivery.

</div>

</div>

# H1 velocity scorecard

| Metric | Q1 | Q2 | H1 view |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Avg completed work items / sprint | 31 | 36 | Throughput up **16%** |
| Median PR cycle time | 5.1 days | 3.8 days | Faster code review and smaller batch size |
| Deployment frequency | 2.1 / week | 3.4 / week | More frequent release cadence |
| Roadmap milestone hit rate | 8 / 10 | 11 / 13 | **83%** overall |
| Rework rate (reopened within 14 days) | 12% | 9% | Quality trend improved |
| On-call interrupt hours / sprint | 23h | 31h | Interrupt load worsened |
| Avg WIP per engineer | 2.8 | 2.3 | Lower WIP helped cycle time |
| Story spillover rate | 18% | 16% | Slight improvement only |

### Readout
- Velocity gains were real, but they came primarily from **smaller change sets and lower WIP**, not from more available engineering capacity.
- Q2 throughput held despite higher interruption because Atlas shifted effort from net-new feature work into reliability and support.

# Constraints likely to affect H2 if unchanged

| Constraint | Evidence from H1 | Likely H2 effect |
|---|---|---|
| Interrupt-driven planning | Q2 unplanned work averaged **23%**; 4 of last 5 sprints were above 20% | Commitment reliability likely remains below target |
| Slow CI and weak test coverage in hotspot services | Build times **28–31 min**; coverage below **55%** in two services | PR cycle time will flatten; deploy frequency gains will stall |
| Aging debt backlog | **46** items older than 180 days; severity concentrated in two services | Change-fail rate remains elevated; incident risk persists |
| On-call concentration | **62%** of incident load handled by 3 senior engineers | Delivery risk and knowledge concentration |
| Feature intake exceeds finish rate in hotspot areas | New feature starts continued while core cleanup remained open | More spillover and more partial work in H2 |

### Assessment
Atlas can likely maintain current output for another quarter, but not improve predictability or safely increase scope without targeted debt reduction.

---

# H2 recommendation: trade some feature capacity for predictability and debt reduction

### Recommendation
Reserve **25% of H2 capacity** for tech debt and reliability work, focused on **Billing Sync** and **Workflow Engine**, and limit mid-sprint intake except for production incidents.

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-8 pt-4">

<div>

### Operating changes
1. **Ring-fence 25% capacity** for debt/remediation in S13–S18.
2. **Freeze net-new work** in Billing Sync for 6 weeks except regulatory or incident-driven changes.
3. Set **WIP cap = 2 active items per engineer** in hotspot services.
4. Route all non-incident intake through a **single weekly triage** with EM approval for mid-sprint additions.
5. Rotate on-call shadowing to reduce senior engineer concentration.

</div>

<div>

### Targets by end of H2
- Commitment reliability: **85% → 88%+**
- Median PR cycle time: **3.8d → ≤3.0d**
- Change fail rate in hotspot services: **18–22% → <10%**
- Open high-severity debt: **37 → <20**
- Build times in hotspot repos: **28–31 min → <15 min**
- Unplanned work share: **23% → <15%**

</div>

</div>

### Decision required
Approve deferral of **2 lower-priority roadmap items** in H2 to fund this plan.  
**Expected tradeoff:** ~**10–12%** lower feature throughput in the near term, in exchange for higher predictability, lower incident risk, and better scaling into Q4.
